Neuro Engineering

Neuroengineering is a rapidly developing area of research in the biomedical and bioengineering fields. Its primary focus is utilizing engineering tools to manipulate the functions of the central, peripheral, and autonomic nervous systems (CNS, PNS & ANS). Neuroengineering aims to create innovative medical technologies that can be used for various purposes, such as screening, diagnosing, prognosis, rehabilitating, repairing, and regenerating neural functions. Among the significant disciplines that have emerged from neuroengineering are the brain-computer interface, deep brain stimulation, and cell replacement therapy. These fields have been established by applying fundamental engineering principles to gain insights into neurological disorders and effectively treat patients suffering from such conditions.

Smart grid (Electrical engineering)

The smart grid in electrical engineering can be summarized as a digital technology that enables seamless two-way communication between the utility provider and its customers while incorporating advanced sensors along the transmission lines. Similar to the concept of the Internet, the Smart Grid integrates controls, computers, automation, and innovative technologies and equipment to respond to the dynamic fluctuations in electric demand collaboratively, ultimately enhancing the efficiency and adaptability of the electrical grid.

Health informatics

Health informatics refers to acquiring, storing, retrieving, and utilizing healthcare information to enhance collaboration among healthcare providers involved in patient care. It plays a vital role in driving healthcare reform, as it facilitates the integration of information technology, communication, and healthcare to elevate the standard and safety of patient care.

Deep mining

As we progress into the future, delving thousands of meters beneath the Earth's surface becomes imperative to access untapped mineral resources. To achieve this, we must continuously advance our knowledge of rock behaviour and refine mining techniques, ensuring the safety and sustainability of these deep mining operations.

Food Technology

Food technology involves utilizing food science principles to carefully handle, process, package, distribute, and ensure the safe consumption of food. This field is closely linked with analytical chemistry, biotechnology, engineering, quality control, nutrition, and food safety management.

Neural Networks

A neural network is a set of algorithms designed to identify underlying relationships in a dataset by simulating the human brain's operations. Neural networks consist of interconnected neurons, either organic or artificial, and can adjust to varying inputs, producing optimal results without requiring output criteria redesign. Rooted in artificial intelligence, neural networks are rapidly gaining popularity in trading system development.

Management Sciences

The problem-solving process involves an interdisciplinary team collaborating to create mathematical models that depict functional relationships ranging from simple to complex. These models serve as a foundation for decision-making, empowering management to make informed choices. Moreover, they facilitate the identification of new problems, thereby enabling quantitative analysis.
